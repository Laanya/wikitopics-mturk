Rate the newsworthiness of Wikipedia articles (PILOT STUDY)

Behind the scenes, we have collected the top 1000 Wikipedia articles for ${date} based on the number of pageviews. You are given 12 of those top 1000. Please help us classify whether the Wikipedia articles relate to current events. Some of these articles are popular because they are newsworthy, meaning reporters and journalists have covered or will want to cover the topic, and some are popular because many people viewed that WIkipedia page on a given day for a more random or abstract reason (such as someone posting it on Reddit or other social websites). You will judge each of the Wikipedia articles listed below for two factors: whether it is newsworthy and current.

Things that count as newsworthy:

  • Major world events like the capture of Osama bin Laden, the Royal Wedding in the UK, or the uprisings in various Arab countries
  • Disasters like the BP oil spill in the Gulf of Mexico or the Japanese earthquake and subsequent tsunami and nuclear disaster
  • Popular culture like blockbuster films, hit TV shows, and current music and related events like the Academy Awards or the Emmys
  • Sports events like the Superbowl, World Series, the Kentucky Derby or the World Cup
  • People in the news like the Navy Seals after they assassinated bin Laden or celebrities/politicians embroiled in scandals
  • Captivating topics like Obama's birth certificate or Harold Camping's prediction of the Rapture

In addition to determining whether each of the Wikipedia articles below corresponds to some recent newsworthy event, we also want to know when it was current relative to ${date}.

We've provided you tools to figure out if an article is newsworthy or not.

1. Click on the Google News link to the article and look for articles around ${date}. If you don't see many articles, then that article probably isn't newsworthy. On the other hand, if there are many articles surrounding a specific or many important events related to the article, please categorize the article as newsworthy.

2. Look at the 'Recent Sentences' column. These are the most recent sentences added to the Wikipedia article, and might contain hints or relevant information that tell us why an article might be newsworthy and current around ${date}.
